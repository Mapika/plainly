# Gold Rewrites — Before / After

Pattern-match against these when fixing. Each shows the technique that turns generic prose
into concrete, varied, human writing.

## From the style authorities

- **Concreteness (Strunk).**
  Before: *A period of unfavorable weather set in.*
  After: *It rained every day for a week.*

- **De-nominalize (Williams).**
  Before: *The intention of the committee is to improve morale.*
  After: *The committee intends to improve morale.*

- **Character as subject (Williams).**
  Before: *Our analysis of the company's performance revealed…*
  After: *We analyzed how the company performed and found…*

- **Cut clutter (Zinsser).**
  *order up → order · tall skyscraper → skyscraper · smile happily → smile · a bit tired → tired*

- **The abstraction anti-model (Orwell's Ecclesiastes parody).**
  Bad (abstract, nominalized, Latinate): *Objective consideration of contemporary phenomena
  compels the conclusion that success or failure in competitive activities exhibits no
  tendency to be commensurate with innate capacity…*
  Good (concrete original): *…the race is not to the swift, nor the battle to the strong…
  but time and chance happeneth to them all.*

- **Rhythm / burstiness (Provost).** Vary sentence length. Short sentences land. Then,
  occasionally, let one sentence run long — building, gathering clauses, carrying the reader
  on its momentum — before you cut back to something short. Like that.

## Per high-weight tell

- **Antithesis "not just X — it's Y"** (weight 3).
  Before: *It's not just a note-taking app — it's a second brain.*
  After: *The app links your notes automatically, so you can find old ideas later.*

- **Trailing significance participle** (weight 3).
  Before: *The company opened three new offices, underscoring its commitment to growth.*
  After: *The company opened three new offices last year, adding 400 jobs in Ohio.*
  (Replace the asserted significance with a fact — or cut the clause.)

- **Booster / puffery** (e.g. *stands as a testament*, weight 3).
  Before: *This groundbreaking tool stands as a testament to the transformative power of innovation.*
  After: *The tool cut our build time from 40 minutes to 6.*

- **Tricolon used as filler** (weight 1; flag when clustered).
  Before: *It's fast, reliable, and scalable.*
  After: *It's fast and cheap — and it hasn't crashed once in six months.*

- **Hollow-profundity ending.**
  Before: *And maybe that's the real lesson here — it was never about the code. It was about the journey.*
  After: *(Cut it, or land a concrete takeaway: "Next time I'll write the tests first.")*

- **Formulaic intro.**
  Before: *In today's fast-paced digital landscape, businesses must adapt to survive.*
  After: *Three of our competitors shut down this year. Here's what they got wrong.*

- **Hedge-stacking.**
  Before: *This approach can often be somewhat more effective in certain situations, though results may vary.*
  After: *This approach is faster. Use it unless you need exact ordering.*
