We moved off Jira on a Tuesday. By Friday, three engineers had told me they'd actually filed bugs they would have skipped before, because the new tool got out of their way.

That tool was Linear. After six years of Jira, our team of fourteen had built a workflow held together by custom fields, four board views nobody fully understood, and a shared dread of the "Create Issue" modal. Sprints took an afternoon to plan. Status meetings ran long because half the time went to arguing about what a ticket's status even meant.

I won't pretend the switch was painless. We lost some history in the migration, and two people missed Jira's reporting for a month. But our cycle time dropped, planning shrank to twenty minutes, and people stopped routing around the tracker entirely.
