For three years, Jira ran our team's work, and for most of those three years, we complained about it. Every sprint planning meeting started with someone wrestling a board into shape. Tickets had fourteen fields and no one filled in twelve of them. Searching for last week's bug felt like an archaeological dig. We told ourselves this was just the cost of doing software, that every tool was bloated and slow once a company got big enough to need it.

Then we tried Linear, and within a week the complaints stopped. Not because Linear is perfect, but because it got out of our way. This post is the honest version of that switch: what actually improved, what we gave up, and the migration headaches we hit along the road. If your team is sitting in the same frustration we were, here is what we learned.
