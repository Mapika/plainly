## Composting for Beginners: Turn Trash into Treasure!

Composting reduces waste and produces rich soil for your garden. It's also much simpler than most people expect.

**What you need:**

- **A compost bin:** Buy one, build one, or just designate a corner of your yard.
- **"Greens":** Nitrogen-rich materials — kitchen scraps (fruit and veggie peels, coffee grounds), grass clippings, garden waste.
- **"Browns":** Carbon-rich materials — dried leaves, shredded paper, cardboard, twigs.

**How to compost:**

1. **Layer it up:** Start with browns on the bottom, then alternate green and brown layers. Aim for a 2-to-1 ratio of browns to greens.
2. **Keep it moist:** The pile should feel like a wrung-out sponge. Add water if it dries out.
3. **Turn it regularly:** Turning every week or two aerates the pile and speeds decomposition.
4. **Be patient:** Finished compost takes anywhere from a few months to a year. It's ready when it's dark, crumbly, and smells like earth.

**Things to avoid:**

- Meat, dairy, and oily foods attract pests and create bad odors.
- Diseased plants can spread disease through the pile.

**Enjoy your compost!**
