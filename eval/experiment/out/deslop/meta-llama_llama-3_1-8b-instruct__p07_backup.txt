**How to Back Up Your Photos Safely**

A device failure, theft, or accident can wipe out years of photos in seconds. A solid backup habit prevents that.

1. **Choose a Backup Method**: Pick an approach that fits how you work.
	* External hard drive: Connect one to your computer and configure it to back up automatically.
	* Cloud storage: Services like Google Photos, iCloud, or Dropbox store your photos offsite, so a house fire won't take them with everything else.
	* USB drive: A simple option for manual transfers, though easy to neglect.

2. **Select a Backup Location**: Your backup is only useful if it survives whatever destroyed the original. A separate computer, a fireproof safe, or an offsite cloud service all put distance between your photos and a single point of failure.

3. **Set Up Automatic Backups**: Manual backups get skipped. Use software like Time Machine or Acronis True Image to run backups on a schedule — weekly is enough for most people, more often if you shoot frequently.

4. **Verify Your Backups**: A backup you've never tested may not work when you need it. Open the backup occasionally and confirm the files are actually there and readable.

Follow these steps and you won't have to find out the hard way that your only copy was on a failing drive.
