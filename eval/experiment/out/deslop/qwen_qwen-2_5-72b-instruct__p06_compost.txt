Starting to compost at home cuts down on waste and gives you free, nutrient-rich soil for the garden. Here's how to begin:

1. **Choose a Composter**: Pick a bin that suits your space—a basic open bin, a tumbler, or a worm bin for tighter spots. Set it somewhere convenient in your yard, out of direct sunlight.

2. **Gather Materials**: You need both "green" and "brown" materials. Greens are fruit and vegetable scraps, coffee grounds, and grass clippings. Browns are dried leaves, twigs, and shredded newspaper. Aim for a 3:1 ratio of browns to greens.

3. **Layer Your Compost**: Start with a layer of browns on the bottom, add a layer of greens, then finish with a thin layer of soil to kick-start decomposition and keep odors down. Keep layering as you accumulate more material.

4. **Maintain Your Compost**: The pile should stay moist but not waterlogged. Turn it every few weeks with a pitchfork or shovel—this aerates the pile, speeds breakdown, and prevents bad smells.

5. **Harvest Your Compost**: After several months, finished compost looks like dark, crumbly soil and smells earthy. Dig it into garden beds, mix it into potting soil, or spread it as a top dressing around plants.

Composting keeps organic waste out of landfills and steadily improves your soil. Happy composting!
