Backing up your photos is the only reliable way to avoid losing them permanently. Here's how to do it:

1. **Choose Your Storage Solution**: Decide where you want to store your backups. External hard drives, cloud services like Google Photos or Dropbox, or both are all solid options.

2. **Organize Your Photos**: Sort your photos into folders by date or event before backing up. This saves a lot of searching later.

3. **Connect Your Device**: Plug your external hard drive into your computer and confirm your system recognizes it.

4. **Copy Photos to External Storage**:
   - **Manual Method**: Drag your photo folders from your computer onto the external drive.
   - **Automated Method**: Use backup software instead. Windows and macOS both include built-in tools for this.

5. **Upload to Cloud Storage**:
   - **Sign Up**: Create an account with a cloud service.
   - **Upload Photos**: Use the app or website to upload your folders. Most services can do this automatically.

6. **Verify the Backup**: Open a few folders and spot-check some photos to confirm everything transferred correctly.

7. **Secure Your Backup**: Keep your external drive somewhere safe—ideally not in the same building as your computer, in case of theft or a fire.

8. **Update Regularly**: Set a monthly reminder, or back up after major events, so your archive stays current.

Follow these steps and you won't have to worry about losing photos to a crashed hard drive or a lost phone.
