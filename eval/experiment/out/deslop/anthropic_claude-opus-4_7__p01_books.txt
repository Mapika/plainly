# Why You Should Read More Books

In an age of endless scrolling and 30-second videos, sitting down with a book can feel almost rebellious. But it might be one of the most rewarding habits you ever develop.

**Reading sharpens your mind.** Studies show that regular reading improves vocabulary, strengthens memory, and slows cognitive decline. Unlike passive entertainment, books force your brain to work—imagining characters, following arguments, connecting ideas across chapters.

**Books expand your world.** A novel can drop you into 19th-century Russia or a distant galaxy. A memoir lets you live another person's life. Nonfiction hands you decades of expertise in a few hundred pages. Few things offer such a generous trade for your time.

**Reading builds empathy.** Spend hours inside a character's thoughts and you come away understanding perspectives unlike your own. In a divided world, that matters.

**It's also genuinely restful.** Six minutes of reading has been shown to reduce stress more effectively than listening to music or taking a walk.

You don't need to commit to a book a week. Ten pages a day adds up to a dozen books a year. Start small.
