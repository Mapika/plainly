Composting is one of the easiest ways to cut down on household waste while producing something genuinely useful for your garden. Here's how to get started.

**What to Compost:**

* Fruit and vegetable scraps
* Tea bags and coffee grounds
* Eggshells
* Yard trimmings and leaves
* Shredded newspaper and cardboard

**What Not to Compost:**

* Meat, dairy, and oils — these attract pests and cause odors
* Pet waste, which can carry disease
* Weeds with seeds, which may sprout in the pile

**Getting Started:**

1. Pick a well-ventilated spot with good drainage for your bin or pile.
2. Build your pile in layers. Start with 4–6 inches of "brown" material — dried leaves or shredded newspaper — then add "green" material like food scraps or grass clippings.
3. Keep the pile about as moist as a wrung-out sponge, and turn it every week or two to speed decomposition.
4. After two to three months, the compost should be dark, crumbly, and ready to work into your garden beds.

The process is slow by nature, so don't expect overnight results. Start with a small pile, add to it regularly, and within a season you'll have a steady supply of rich amendment that noticeably improves your soil.
