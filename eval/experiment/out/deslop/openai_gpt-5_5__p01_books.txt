## Why You Should Read More Books

In a world of quick videos, endless scrolling, and constant notifications, reading books can feel almost old-fashioned. But that is exactly why it matters.

Books offer something most modern habits do not: depth. A good book asks you to slow down and think. Whether it is fiction, history, or biography, reading lets you engage with ideas far more fully than a headline ever could.

Reading also strengthens the mind. It builds vocabulary, concentration, and memory. Fiction lets you see the world through someone else's eyes. Nonfiction teaches practical skills, introduces new perspectives, and sharpens judgment.

Books are also a genuine escape. After a stressful day, reading tends to be calming in a way that screens are not. A novel can take you somewhere else entirely. A thoughtful essay can make you feel less alone.

Most importantly, reading is an investment in yourself. You do not need to finish a book a week or push through every one you start. Just read a little more than you do now. A few pages a day can open doors that were not there before.
