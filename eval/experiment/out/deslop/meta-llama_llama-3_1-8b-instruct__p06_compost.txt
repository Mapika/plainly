**Starting a Home Composting Guide for Beginners**

Composting turns kitchen and yard waste into rich soil amendment with minimal effort. Here's how to get started.

**What to Compost:**

* Fruit and vegetable peels and scraps
* Tea bags and coffee grounds
* Eggshells
* Bread and grains
* Yard trimmings and leaves
* Avoid meat, dairy, and oily foods

**Setting Up Your Compost:**

1. Pick a shaded spot with decent airflow for your bin.
2. Choose a container that fits your space — plastic, wood, or wire mesh all work.
3. Lay down 4–6 inches of brown material (dry leaves, straw, or shredded newspaper) at the bottom.

**Adding Materials:**

1. Alternate "green" layers (food scraps, grass clippings) with brown ones.
2. Keep the pile about as moist as a wrung-out sponge.
3. Turn the contents every week or two so air can reach the center.

**Tips and Maintenance:**

* Check the temperature — an active pile should hit 130–140°F.
* Add water if the pile feels dry.
* Don't overload the bin; airflow matters.
* After 2–3 months, compost is ready when it looks dark and crumbly and smells like soil.

Expect a wait of a few months, but routine turning and occasional watering are all it takes to get there.
