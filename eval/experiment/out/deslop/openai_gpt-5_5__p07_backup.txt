## How to Back Up Your Photos Safely

Backing up your photos protects them from phone loss, computer failure, accidental deletion, or theft. A good rule is the **3-2-1 backup method**: keep **three copies** of your photos, on **two different types of storage**, with **one copy stored off-site**.

Start by gathering your photos from your phone, camera, and computer into one folder. Sort them by year, event, or month so they're easy to find later.

Next, make a local backup. Copy your photo folder to an **external hard drive** or SSD, then open a few files to confirm the transfer worked. Keep the drive somewhere safe and update it regularly.

Then set up cloud backup through a service like Google Photos, iCloud, OneDrive, or Dropbox. Enable automatic uploads so new photos are saved without any effort on your part.

For extra protection, store a second external drive somewhere outside your home — a relative's house or another secure location. This covers you if fire, flood, or theft hits your primary location.

Finally, check your backups every few months. Confirm files still open, cloud syncing is active, and storage isn't full. A backup you haven't verified is one you can't rely on.
