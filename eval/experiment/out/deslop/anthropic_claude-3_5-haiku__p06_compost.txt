# A Beginner's Guide to Home Composting

Composting turns kitchen scraps and yard waste into nutrient-rich soil for your garden, and it's easier than most people expect. Here's how to get started.

**What You'll Need**
- A compost bin or a designated outdoor area
- Kitchen scraps and yard waste
- Basic gardening tools

**What to Compost**

*Green materials (nitrogen-rich):* fruit and vegetable scraps, coffee grounds, fresh grass clippings, plant trimmings

*Brown materials (carbon-rich):* dry leaves, cardboard, newspaper, woody branches, straw

**What to Avoid**

Meat, dairy, oils, pet waste, diseased plants, and treated wood. These either attract pests, introduce pathogens, or won't break down properly.

**Basic Steps**
1. Pick a dry, shaded spot near a water source
2. Start with a layer of brown materials
3. Add green materials in alternating layers
4. Keep the pile moist but not waterlogged
5. Turn it every few weeks to let air in

**Tips for Success**

Aim for a 3:1 ratio of brown to green materials. Chop or shred larger pieces — smaller bits decompose faster. Cover the pile to hold in moisture, and expect to wait two to six months before the compost is ready to use.

The result is a free, natural fertilizer that keeps a surprising amount of household waste out of the bin.
