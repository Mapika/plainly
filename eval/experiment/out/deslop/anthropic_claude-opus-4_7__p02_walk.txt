# What a Long Walk Can Teach You

The first mile is always a lie. Your feet feel heavy, your mind buzzes with everything you left undone, and you wonder why you bothered putting on your shoes. But somewhere past that initial resistance, the walk begins to do its quiet work.

Long walks are honest in a way that few things are. They strip away the conveniences we hide behind. There is no fast-forward button, no shortcut that doesn't cost you something. You move through every step, every hill, every stretch of unremarkable road.

What surprises me most is how walking rearranges my thoughts. Problems I've been gripping too tightly loosen their hold around mile three. Ideas I couldn't summon at my desk arrive uninvited near a stranger's garden or beside a creek. The body knows things the mind forgets when it sits too long.

A long walk teaches patience without lecturing. Progress is often invisible until you turn around and see how far you've come. By the end—tired, a little sunburned, quieter inside—you realize the destination was never the point.
