## Ditch the Scroll, Grab a Scroll (of Paper)!

It's easy to forget what a good book can do when you're drowning in digital content. Why carve out time for reading when your schedule is already packed? Because it makes you sharper, more knowledgeable, and better at understanding other people.

Books take you to places you've never been — different cultures, historical periods, perspectives far removed from your own — without leaving your armchair. They challenge assumptions you didn't know you had and stretch your imagination in ways a feed never will.

Reading is also genuinely good for your brain. It builds focus and memory, strengthens critical thinking, and research consistently links it to lower stress and better sleep.

The strongest case for reading, though, might be what it does for your relationships. When you spend hours inside a character's life — their fears, their failures, their small victories — you come away more capable of extending that same understanding to real people. That's not a soft benefit. It changes how you show up at work, at home, and in difficult conversations.

So put the phone down, pick up a book, and see where it takes you.
