**Beginner's Guide to Home Composting**

Starting a compost bin at home cuts down on waste and produces nutrient-rich soil for your garden. Here's how to begin:

1. **Choose a Bin**: Pick a compost bin that fits your space. Buy one, or build your own from wood pallets or wire mesh. A simple pile in a corner of the yard also works.

2. **Gather Materials**: Compostable materials fall into two categories: greens and browns. Greens include kitchen scraps — fruit and vegetable peels, coffee grounds, eggshells. Browns are dry leaves, cardboard, and straw. Getting the balance right matters for healthy compost.

3. **Layering**: Start with a layer of browns, then add greens on top. Aim for roughly 3 parts browns to 1 part greens.

4. **Aerate**: Turn your compost every few weeks with a pitchfork or shovel. This introduces oxygen and speeds up decomposition.

5. **Moisture**: Keep your compost moist but not soggy. Too dry? Add water. Too wet? Add more browns.

6. **Patience**: Composting takes time. Within a few months, you'll have dark, crumbly compost ready to work into your garden soil.

Happy composting!
