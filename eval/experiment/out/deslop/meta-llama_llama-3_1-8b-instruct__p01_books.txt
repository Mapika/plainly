**Get Lost in the Pages: Why Reading More Books Matters**

In the grind of daily life, it's easy to forget the pleasure of sitting down with a good book. That's a shame, because reading regularly does more than pass the time. It builds vocabulary, sharpens how you communicate, and has a way of shifting how you see the world—sometimes in ways you didn't expect.

A good book can drop you into a different century, a foreign country, or a mind nothing like your own. Fiction and non-fiction alike do this, just differently. Beyond broadening your perspective, reading is also one of the more effective ways to decompress. Screens stimulate; books tend to slow things down.

The case for reading more isn't complicated. Pick a time that works—morning coffee, a lunch break, the hour before bed—and protect it. The books are there. The only real obstacle is starting.
