Composting at home cuts waste and builds rich soil for your garden. Here's how to start:

**1. Choose Your Composter:**
A tumbler or a three-sided bin in a shady spot works well for beginners. A simple open pile is fine too.

**2. Gather Your Ingredients:**
Compost needs "greens" (nitrogen-rich materials like fruit and vegetable scraps, coffee grounds, and grass clippings) and "browns" (carbon-rich materials like dry leaves, shredded paper, and small twigs). Aim for roughly equal parts by volume.

**3. Layer It Up:**
Start with browns, add greens on top, and keep alternating. Chop larger pieces to speed up decomposition.

**4. Keep It Moist and Aerated:**
The pile should feel like a wrung-out sponge. Water it if it dries out. Turn it every week or two with a pitchfork — or rotate your tumbler — to let air in and keep the microbes active.

**5. What NOT to Compost:**
Skip meat, dairy, and oily foods, which attract pests. Also avoid pet waste, which carries pathogens, and diseased plants.

Within a few months you'll have dark, earthy-smelling compost ready to work into your garden beds.
