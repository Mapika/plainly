### **Composting for Beginners: Turn Scraps into Soil Gold**

Composting is nature's recycling system — a straightforward process of turning kitchen scraps and yard waste into rich fertilizer for your plants.

**1. Choose Your Bin:** Start with a simple enclosed compost bin for your yard or a countertop tumbler. Short on space? A sealed bokashi bin or a worm farm (vermicomposting) can work indoors.

**2. Know What to Add:** A healthy compost pile needs a balance of "greens" and "browns."
*   **Greens (Nitrogen):** Fruit and vegetable scraps, coffee grounds, fresh grass clippings.
*   **Browns (Carbon):** Dried leaves, cardboard, straw, paper egg cartons, shredded newspaper.

**3. What to Avoid:** Never add meat, dairy, oils, or diseased plants. They attract pests and produce foul odors.

**4. Simple Steps:** Layer your greens and browns in the bin. Chop larger pieces to speed decomposition. Keep the pile about as moist as a wrung-out sponge and turn it with a pitchfork every week or two to let air in.

Within a few months you'll have dark, crumbly compost to mix into your garden soil. Your plants get a nutrient boost, and less waste ends up in the landfill.
