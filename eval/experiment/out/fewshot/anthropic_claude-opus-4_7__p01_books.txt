**On the Quiet Habit of Reading**

I won't pretend reading is some noble act that will fix your life. People say that sort of thing about kale and meditation, and I don't believe them about those either. But there is something to be said for sitting down with a book.

Most of what we consume now is designed to be consumed quickly and then forgotten — scrolled, glanced at, half-watched while we do something else. A book asks more of you. It asks you to stay in one place, to follow a thought to its end, to let someone else's mind run alongside yours for a few hours. That's a strange and increasingly rare thing.

You also get to be quiet. Not silent, exactly, because a good book is noisy in its own way, but quiet on the outside. No one is talking at you. No one is trying to sell you anything (well, sometimes the author is, but at least they're working for it).

I'm not going to tell you it'll make you smarter or kinder or richer. It might. Mostly, though, it will give you back something the rest of the day keeps taking — your attention. Which, when you think about it, is most of what you have.
