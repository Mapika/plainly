The bibliophile is a forlorn and sorry-looking creature, with a dusty old book clutched in its grasp, a hankering for knowledge that forever sags down with a desperate expression of longing and intellectual poverty. Its eyes are furtive and bookish, scanning the shelves with a mixture of hope and despair, searching for the next great read. Its face is a long, worn expanse, with a furrowed brow and a look of studious concentration. It is a living, breathing embodiment of Curiosity, always seeking, always learning, always hungry for more.

But the meanest of creatures - the procrastinator, the TV addict, the social media zombie - despises the bibliophile, and even the most trivial of distractions would desert it for a fleeting moment of mindless entertainment. It is so spiritless and aimless that even while its eyes are scanning the pages, the rest of its face is apologizing for not having read more.

But the truth is, the more you read, the more you'll want to read. The process is reversible. Your thoughts will become clearer, your mind will become sharper, and your language will become more precise. So take up a book, dear reader, and let the words wash over you. Your mind - and your soul - will thank you.
