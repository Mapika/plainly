So, you've got a phone full of memories, a camera bulging with holiday snaps, and the nagging fear that one slip, one stolen device, one rogue cup of coffee, and poof – they're gone. Vanished. A digital ghost. Don't be that person, the one staring blankly at a black screen, muttering about "should have."

Backing up your photos isn't rocket science, just good sense. First, pick a cloud service. Google Photos, iCloud, Dropbox – they're all pretty good at hoovering up your pictures and keeping them safe in some digital ether. Set it to auto-sync, and forget about it. That's your first line of defense.

But don't stop there. The internet is a fickle beast. Get yourself an external hard drive, a solid, chunky thing that sits on your desk like a silent guardian. Drag and drop those precious files onto it. Do this regularly, once a month, once a week, whatever feels right. Think of it as putting your photos in a vault. And for the truly paranoid (or just wise), consider a third backup, perhaps another cloud service, or a second hard drive stored off-site. Because when it comes to memories, there's no such thing as too safe.
