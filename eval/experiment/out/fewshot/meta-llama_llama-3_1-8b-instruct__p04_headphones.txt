The AuraPods Pro are a sorry excuse for a pair of headphones, a haphazard collection of wires and plastic held together by a prayer and a shoestring. They're a pair of flaccid, over-sized blobs that look like they were designed by a committee of tone-deaf accountants, with a " premium" finish that's about as convincing as a used car salesman's smile. But, somehow, they work. The noise-cancelling tech is a marvel, a magical elixir that makes the world outside melt away like a mirage on a hot summer day. The sound quality is a mixed bag, a jumbled mess of highs and lows that's about as coherent as a teenager's thoughts on a Sunday morning. But, hey, at least they're wireless. You can wear them while you're walking down the street, or riding a unicycle, or trying to remember where you put your keys. They're the perfect accessory for the modern, aimless, distracted human.
