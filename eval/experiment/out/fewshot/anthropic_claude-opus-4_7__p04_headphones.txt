Look, you've heard the pitch before. "Immersive sound." "Crystal-clear calls." "Premium materials." Every set of headphones on the shelf claims the same things, and most of them are lying or, at best, exaggerating wildly.

The AuraPods Pro probably exaggerate too, but here's what we can actually tell you: they're light enough that you'll forget you're wearing them around hour three, the noise cancellation genuinely flattens the rumble of a subway car, and the battery has lasted us about thirty hours between charges, give or take depending on volume. The ear cushions are made of a memory foam that doesn't get sweaty in the way cheaper pairs do after a long flight.

They aren't magic. They won't fix a bad recording or make your commute pleasant. But put them on, press play, and the world thins out a little. For most people, most days, that's enough.

$299. Black or sand.
