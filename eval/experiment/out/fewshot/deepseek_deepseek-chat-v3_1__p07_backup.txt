First, stop trusting luck with your photos. That phone will be lost, that laptop will fail. Your job is to create copies, plural, before fate intervenes.

Start with the **3-2-1 Rule**: keep **three** total copies, on **two** different types of media, with **one** copy offsite. Here’s what that looks like in practice.

**One:** Your originals live on your main device. **Two:** Immediately back them up to an external hard drive you keep at home. Plug it in weekly, let your computer do the rest automatically. This is your quick-salvage copy.

**Three:** This is the lifesaver—the offsite copy. Use a cloud service like Google Photos, iCloud, or Dropbox. Set it to upload automatically from your phone and computer. Yes, you’re trusting a corporation, but you’re also guarding against a house fire or theft that would take both your device and that local hard drive.

Finally, don’t just stash them and forget. Once a year, open that cloud album and scroll. Make sure it’s working. Bask in a memory. That’s the whole point—to know your moments are safe, so you can actually enjoy them.
