The modern office is a curious creature, with its rows of sleek workstations and its seemingly endless expanse of empty space. And at the heart of it all, the open-plan office, a beast of a concept that's supposed to foster collaboration and creativity, but in reality only serves to suffocate the soul. It's a place where the faint scent of burnt coffee and stale air hangs heavy, where the hum of printers and the chatter of coworkers blend into a maddening din, and where the very notion of personal space is reduced to a distant memory.

But, you may ask, what's the harm in a little openness? Won't it bring us all closer together, facilitate the free flow of ideas, and make us more productive? Alas, the evidence suggests otherwise. Studies have shown that open-plan offices lead to increased stress levels, decreased productivity, and a general sense of discontent. And it's no wonder, really – when everyone's elbow is in your ear, it's hard to focus on anything except the coworker who's snacking on their lunch at 2 PM. So, let's be honest, shall we? The open-plan office is less a symbol of modernity and more a relic of a bygone era, a misguided attempt to create a "community" that's really just a bunch of overcaffeinated strangers squished together in a windowless room.
