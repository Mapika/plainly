Here’s a short blog post on why people should read more books, in a similarly human style:

Let's face it, our screens are glued to our palms, feeding us a constant drip-feed of fleeting information. We scroll, we tap, we double-tap, and then… what? A vague sense of having consumed something, perhaps, but rarely a feeling of digestion. This, my friends, is where books swagger in, like seasoned adventurers returning from a grand quest.

Reading a book isn't just about absorbing words; it's a deep dive. It's a workout for your brain, stretching those mental muscles you didn't even know you had. You’re not just passively receiving; you’re engaging, imagining, building entire worlds inside your head. It teaches you patience, empathy, and the glorious art of sustained attention – a rare and precious commodity in our hyper-caffeinated age. So, ditch the endless scroll, pick up a book, and let your mind truly feast. You’ll be smarter, sharper, and frankly, a much more interesting human being for it.
