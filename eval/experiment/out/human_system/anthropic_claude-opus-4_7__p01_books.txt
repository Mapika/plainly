# Read More Books. Seriously.

I know, I know. You've heard this before. Your high school English teacher said it, your dad says it, every productivity blogger says it. So let me try a different angle.

Books are the only medium left that demands your full attention for hours at a stretch. That's the whole point. Not the vocabulary boost, not the "expanding your horizons" line — the sustained focus. Reading a novel for two hours rewires something that scrolling for two hours quietly dismantles.

You've probably noticed it yourself. You sit down to read and your eyes keep skating off the page after a paragraph. That's not a character flaw. That's a trained reflex from years of swiping. The good news: it comes back. Give it a week of twenty minutes a night and you'll feel the difference.

Pick something you actually want to read, not something you think you should read. A trashy thriller counts. A weird memoir from 1978 counts. The literary canon will still be there next year if you decide to visit.

Put your phone in another room. Open the book. That's the whole practice.
