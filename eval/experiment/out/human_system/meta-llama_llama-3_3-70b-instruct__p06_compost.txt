Start with a bin. You can buy one or make your own using a wooden pallet or an old trash can. Drill some holes in it for air and water to get in. Choose a spot for your bin that's easy to get to and out of direct sunlight. 

Add brown things like dried leaves, shredded paper, or small twigs to your bin. These provide carbon. Then add green things like food scraps, grass clippings, or manure from pets. These provide nitrogen. Aim for a mix of two-thirds brown and one-third green. 

Add your scraps gradually, mixing them in as you go. Don't add meat, dairy, or oils - they attract pests and smell bad. Eggshells and coffee grounds are great, though. Every few days, use a pitchfork to turn the pile and get some air in. This helps it break down faster. 

Keep the pile moist, like a damp sponge. If it's too dry, nothing will happen. If it's too wet, it'll smell. After a few months, you'll have rich, dark compost to add to your garden or houseplants. It's a slow process, but it's worth it for the free, nutrient-rich soil it gives you.
