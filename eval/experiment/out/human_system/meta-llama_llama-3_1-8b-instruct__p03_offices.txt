Open-plan offices have been touted as the epitome of modern workspaces, fostering collaboration and creativity among employees. But scratch beneath the surface and you'll find a different story. The constant din of conversation, the staccato clacking of keyboards, and the constant distractions make it impossible to focus on individual tasks.

Studies have shown that employees who work in open-plan offices experience higher levels of stress, anxiety, and burnout. The constant interruptions and lack of personal space take a toll on productivity, leading to decreased motivation and job satisfaction. And let's not forget the noise – a recent study found that open-plan offices can be as loud as a busy road, with noise levels exceeding 70 decibels.

But perhaps the most insidious effect of open-plan offices is the erosion of personal boundaries. When everyone is in close proximity, it's impossible to escape the constant chatter, the gossip, and the watercooler politics. The office becomes a social hierarchy, where those who can shout the loudest get heard, and the introverts are left struggling to be seen. It's time to rethink the open-plan office model and prioritize individual space and quiet contemplation. Not because it's "traditional" or "old-fashioned," but because it's what works best for most people.
