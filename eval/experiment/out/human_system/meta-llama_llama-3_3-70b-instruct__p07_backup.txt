To back up your photos safely, start by gathering all your devices - phones, cameras, tablets. Connect them to your computer and transfer all your photos to one place. Use a folder system that makes sense to you, like sorting by year or event.

Next, choose an external hard drive. Look for one with enough space to hold all your photos, and consider one that's rugged and portable. Plug it into your computer and drag your photos folder onto the drive. This will create a copy of your photos outside of your computer.

For extra safety, use a cloud storage service like Google Photos or Dropbox. Upload your photos to the cloud, and make sure to set the service to automatically sync any new photos you take. This way, even if your computer and external drive are damaged or lost, your photos will still be safe online.

Finally, make sure to check your backups regularly to ensure they're complete and up-to-date. Set a reminder to back up your photos every few months, and consider storing a copy of your external drive in a separate location, like a safe deposit box. This will give you peace of mind knowing your memories are safe.
