FreshCrate's food arrives in a compact, insulated box. Inside, meals are portioned into reusable containers, wrapped in paper bags. A separate pouch contains recipes and ingredient lists printed on cardstock. The system aims to minimize waste and excess packaging. Meals are designed to serve one or two people, with ingredients and instructions for each dinner of the week. FreshCrate's menu changes seasonally, with an overall focus on fresh vegetables, whole grains, and lean proteins. Meals are often international in inspiration, drawing from Mediterranean, Asian, and Latin American cuisines. Some meals are vegetarian or vegan, while others include fish or poultry. FreshCrate's recipes are developed by in-house chefs, who aim to balance flavors and textures in each dish. The subscription starts at $60 per week, with options to skip or cancel at any time. FreshCrate operates in most major US cities, with delivery on weekdays.
