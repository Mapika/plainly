Open-plan offices are often touted as a way to foster collaboration and creativity, but I'm not convinced. I've worked in a few, and the constant din of conversation and clacking keyboards is distracting, to say the least. It's hard to focus when someone nearby is on a loud phone call or a group is brainstorming at full volume. And don't even get me started on the lack of privacy – try making a personal call or discussing a sensitive project in a room full of colleagues.

Proponents of open-plan offices claim they encourage teamwork and innovation, but I think this is a myth. In reality, people often end up wearing headphones or retreating to quiet rooms to get any real work done. And what about introverts, who may find the constant stimulation exhausting? A well-designed office should accommodate different work styles, not try to force everyone into a one-size-fits-all mold. Give me a quiet, private space to work any day – I'll be more productive, and happier, as a result. The open-plan office may look trendy and modern, but it's a fad that's due for a rethink.
