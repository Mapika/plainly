The open-plan office: a symbol of modern work. But is it actually any good? I don't think so.

The theory is that open plans foster collaboration. People bump into each other, ideas spark, creativity flows! The reality is more like: constant low-level noise, distracting conversations, and the creeping feeling that you're always being watched.

Focus becomes a precious commodity, fought for with noise-cancelling headphones and desperate trips to the nearest empty meeting room. Introverts, who often need quiet to think, suffer most. But even extroverts can find it hard to concentrate when surrounded by constant activity.

Proponents argue that open plans save money on real estate. Maybe. But what's the cost of reduced productivity and increased employee stress? A few dollars saved on rent hardly seems worth it.

There are alternatives. Hybrid models, with a mix of open areas and private spaces, offer a better balance. Let people choose where they work best. Give them the autonomy to manage their own focus. That's a better way to foster collaboration than forcing everyone into the same noisy room.
