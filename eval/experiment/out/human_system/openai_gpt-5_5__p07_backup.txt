Start with the 3-2-1 rule: keep three copies of your photos, on two different kinds of storage, with one copy off-site. This protects you from a stolen laptop, a dead hard drive, or a cloud account problem.

First, gather your photos in one main place. On a computer, create a folder called “Photos” and sort by year and month if you can. Copy in pictures from your phone, camera cards, old laptops, and messaging apps.

Next, turn on cloud backup. Good options include iCloud Photos, Google Photos, OneDrive, or Amazon Photos. Check the settings carefully. You want full-resolution backup, not a smaller “storage saver” copy if image quality matters to you.

Then make a local backup. Buy an external hard drive or SSD with more space than you currently need. Once a week, plug it in and copy your photo folder to it, or use built-in backup software like Time Machine on Mac or File History on Windows.

Keep another copy away from your home. This can be a second drive stored at a trusted relative’s house or a cloud backup service.

Finally, test your backups. Open a few old photos from each place. A backup you never check is a guess.
