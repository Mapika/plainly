The best thinking I do happens on foot. I don't mean the kind of thinking that solves problems. That kind needs a desk and quiet. I mean the kind that rearranges your soul.

A long walk scrapes away the accumulated gunk of the day. The endless notifications, the low-grade anxieties, the feeling of being perpetually behind — they fade with each step. Walking pulls you into the present. You notice the way sunlight hits a brick wall, the sound of wind in the trees.

It’s a chance to eavesdrop on yourself. Thoughts that were buried bubble to the surface. You realize what you're truly angry about, what you're longing for, what you need to forgive. The rhythm of walking is like a drumbeat that loosens the tongue of your subconscious.

The world seems different when you're moving through it at three miles an hour. You see details you'd miss from a car. You connect with the place. A long walk is a pilgrimage to the self. When you return, you are lighter, clearer, more at home in your own skin.
