To back up your photos safely, start by sorting them into folders by date or event. This will make it easier to identify and retrieve specific photos later. Choose a backup location that's separate from your primary storage device – a USB drive or external hard drive is ideal.

For the most robust backup, use a 3-2-1 approach: three copies of your data, on two different types of media, with one copy stored offsite. This means keeping your original files on your computer, a second copy on an external drive, and a third copy in the cloud. Consider using a cloud service like Google Photos or iCloud, which can automatically upload new photos and provide access from anywhere.

When using an external drive, be sure to verify the backup by checking that all files have been copied correctly. This is especially important if you're using a USB drive, as they can be prone to data corruption. Finally, make it a habit to regularly review and update your backup to ensure it's up to date. Set a reminder to check your backup every few months, and update your cloud storage accordingly. This will give you peace of mind knowing your memories are safe.
