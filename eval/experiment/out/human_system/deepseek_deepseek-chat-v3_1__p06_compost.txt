Start composting. It will turn your kitchen scraps into rich, black soil. You’re not throwing things away; you’re building a new resource.

First, choose a simple container. A lidded plastic bin with a few holes drilled in the bottom for drainage is perfect for a balcony or yard. Place it in a shady spot.

Now, feed it a balanced diet. Add "greens" for nitrogen and "browns" for carbon. Greens are your fruit and vegetable peels, coffee grounds, and fresh grass clippings. Browns are dried leaves, shredded cardboard, and egg cartons. For every handful of wet, smelly greens, add two handfuls of dry, bulky browns. This prevents a slimy, rotting mess.

Chop your scraps into smaller pieces. They’ll break down faster. Every week or two, give the pile a stir with a shovel or stick to let in air. It should feel like a damp sponge. Add water if it’s dry, or more browns if it’s soggy.

In a few months, you’ll find dark, crumbly compost at the bottom. It won’t smell like garbage. It will smell like a forest after rain. Mix it into your garden soil or potted plants. Watch everything grow better. You’ve just closed the loop.
