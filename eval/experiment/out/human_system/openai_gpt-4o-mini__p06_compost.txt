Starting a compost pile at home is a simple and rewarding way to reduce waste and create nutrient-rich soil for your garden. First, choose a location. Ideally, find a spot in your yard that’s dry, shady, and easy to access. If you don’t have a yard, consider a compact bin for your balcony or kitchen scraps.

Next, gather materials. Compost needs a mix of ‘greens’ and ‘browns.’ Greens are nitrogen-rich items like fruit and vegetable scraps, coffee grounds, and grass clippings. Browns are carbon-rich materials such as dried leaves, cardboard, and shredded newspaper. Aim for a ratio of about 1 part greens to 3 parts browns.

Layer these materials in your compost pile. Start with a layer of browns to help with drainage, then add greens, and continue alternating. Turn the pile every few weeks with a pitchfork or shovel to aerate it and speed up decomposition. 

Keep the pile moist, like a damp sponge, but not soggy. In a few months, you’ll see the compost break down into dark, crumbly soil. Use this rich compost in your garden or potted plants to nourish them naturally. Happy composting!
