Starting a compost pile might seem daunting, but it’s really quite simple. You’re just mimicking nature's process of decay. First, find a good spot. A shaded area, not too close to your house, is ideal. You can buy a compost bin, or make one with wire mesh or old pallets.

Next, gather your materials. You need a mix of "greens" (nitrogen-rich) and "browns" (carbon-rich). Greens include fruit and vegetable scraps, coffee grounds, and fresh grass clippings. Browns are things like dry leaves, wood chips, shredded paper, and straw. Aim for roughly equal amounts, or slightly more browns.

Layer these materials in your bin. Start with a layer of browns, then add greens, then more browns. Keep the pile moist, like a wrung-out sponge, but not soaking wet. Too much water will make it smelly. Turn the pile every week or two with a pitchfork to aerate it. This speeds up decomposition. In a few months, you’ll have rich, dark compost, perfect for your garden.
