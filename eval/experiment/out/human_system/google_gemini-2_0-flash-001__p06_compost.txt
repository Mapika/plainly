Want to turn kitchen scraps into garden gold? Composting at home is easier than you think.

First, you need a container. A simple plastic bin with a lid works. Drill some air holes for ventilation. Now, start collecting "greens" (nitrogen-rich stuff like fruit and veggie peels, coffee grounds, and grass clippings) and "browns" (carbon-rich stuff like dried leaves, shredded paper, and cardboard).

The key is balance. Aim for roughly twice as much brown as green. Layer them in your bin: start with a layer of browns, then add greens. Moisten the pile so it's damp as a wrung-out sponge. Too wet? Add more browns. Too dry? Spritz with water.

Turn the compost every few days to aerate it. This speeds up decomposition. You can use a garden fork or shovel.

Avoid composting meat, dairy, oily foods, and diseased plants. These attract pests and can create odors.

In a few months, you’ll have dark, crumbly compost. Mix it into your garden soil to enrich it. Congratulations — you're a composter!
