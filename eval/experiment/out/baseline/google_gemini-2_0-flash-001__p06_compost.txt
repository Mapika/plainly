## Composting for Beginners: Turn Trash into Treasure!

Composting is a fantastic way to reduce waste and create nutrient-rich soil for your garden. Don't be intimidated, it's easier than you think!

**What you need:**

*   **A compost bin:** You can buy one or make your own! Even a pile in a corner of your yard works.
*   **"Greens":** Nitrogen-rich materials like kitchen scraps (fruit & veggie peels, coffee grounds), grass clippings, and garden waste.
*   **"Browns":** Carbon-rich materials like dried leaves, shredded paper, cardboard, and twigs.

**How to compost:**

1.  **Layer it up:** Start with a layer of browns at the bottom. Then alternate layers of greens and browns. Aim for a ratio of 2-3 parts browns to 1 part greens.
2.  **Keep it moist:** Your compost should be damp, like a wrung-out sponge. Add water if it's too dry.
3.  **Turn it regularly:** Turning your compost pile every week or two helps aerate it, speeding up decomposition.
4.  **Be patient:** It can take a few months to a year for your compost to be ready. You'll know it's done when it's dark, crumbly, and smells earthy.

**Things to avoid:**

*   Meat, dairy, and oily foods attract pests and create unpleasant odors.
*   Diseased plants can spread disease.

**Enjoy your nutrient-rich compost in your garden!**
