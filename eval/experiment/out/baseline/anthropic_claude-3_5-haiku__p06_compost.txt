A Beginner's Guide to Home Composting

Composting is an easy and eco-friendly way to reduce waste and create nutrient-rich soil for your garden. Here's how to get started:

What You'll Need:
- A compost bin or designated outdoor area
- Kitchen scraps
- Yard waste
- Basic gardening tools

What to Compost:
Green materials (nitrogen-rich):
- Fruit and vegetable scraps
- Coffee grounds
- Fresh grass clippings
- Plant trimmings

Brown materials (carbon-rich):
- Dry leaves
- Cardboard
- Newspaper
- Woody branches
- Straw

What to Avoid:
- Meat and dairy products
- Diseased plants
- Oils
- Pet waste
- Treated wood

Basic Steps:
1. Choose a location: Select a dry, shaded spot near a water source
2. Start with a layer of brown materials
3. Add green materials in alternating layers
4. Keep the pile moist but not soaked
5. Turn the compost every few weeks to aerate

Tips for Success:
- Aim for a 3:1 ratio of brown to green materials
- Chop materials into smaller pieces for faster decomposition
- Cover the pile to retain moisture
- Be patient - composting takes 2-6 months

With a little effort, you'll soon create rich, natural fertilizer while reducing household waste!
