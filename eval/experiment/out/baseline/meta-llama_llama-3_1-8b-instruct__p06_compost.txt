**Starting a Home Composting Guide for Beginners**

Composting is a simple and effective way to reduce waste, create nutrient-rich soil, and help the environment. Here's a step-by-step guide to get you started:

**What to Compost:**

* Fruit and vegetable peels and scraps
* Tea bags and coffee grounds
* Eggshells
* Bread and grains
* Yard trimmings and leaves
* Avoid meat, dairy, and oily foods

**Setting Up Your Compost:**

1. Choose a location for your compost bin, ideally in a shaded area with good airflow.
2. Select a bin or container that suits your space and needs (e.g., plastic, wooden, or wire mesh).
3. Add a 4-6 inch layer of brown materials (dry leaves, straw, or shredded newspaper) at the bottom of the bin.

**Adding Materials:**

1. Alternate layers of "green" (food scraps, grass clippings) and "brown" materials.
2. Keep the compost moist, like a damp sponge.
3. Mix the contents every week or two to aerate the pile.

**Tips and Maintenance:**

* Monitor temperature (it should reach 130-140°F).
* Add water as needed.
* Avoid overloading the bin.
* Harvest your compost after 2-3 months, when it's dark, crumbly, and has an earthy scent.

Remember, patience is key. With regular maintenance and care, you'll be enjoying the benefits of composting in no time!
