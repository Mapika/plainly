Composting at home is an eco-friendly way to reduce waste and create nutrient-rich soil for your plants! Here’s how to get started:

**1. Choose Your Composter:**
For beginners, a simple tumbler or a three-sided bin in a shady spot works well. You can also create a basic pile.

**2. Gather Your Ingredients:**
Compost needs a balance of "greens" (nitrogen-rich, like fruit/veg scraps, coffee grounds, grass clippings) and "browns" (carbon-rich, like dry leaves, shredded paper, small twigs). Aim for roughly equal parts by volume.

**3. Layer It Up:**
Start with a layer of browns, then add greens, and continue alternating. Chop larger items into smaller pieces to speed up decomposition.

**4. Keep it Moist and Aerated:**
Your compost should feel like a damp sponge. Water if it’s too dry. Turn your pile every week or two with a pitchfork or by rotating your tumbler to introduce air, which helps the microbes work their magic.

**5. What NOT to Compost:**
Avoid meat, dairy, oily foods (attract pests), pet waste (contains pathogens), and diseased plants.

Within a few months, you'll have beautiful, dark, earthy-smelling compost ready to enrich your garden!
