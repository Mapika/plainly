## How to Back Up Your Photos Safely

Backing up your photos protects them from phone loss, computer failure, accidental deletion, or theft. A good rule is the **3-2-1 backup method**: keep **three copies** of your photos, on **two different types of storage**, with **one copy stored off-site**.

First, gather your photos from your phone, camera, and computer into one organized folder. Sort them by year, event, or month so they are easy to find later.

Next, make a local backup. Copy your photo folder to an **external hard drive** or SSD. After copying, open a few files to make sure the backup worked. Keep this drive somewhere safe, and update it regularly.

Then, create a cloud backup using a trusted service such as Google Photos, iCloud, OneDrive, Dropbox, or another secure provider. Turn on automatic photo uploads so new pictures are backed up without you needing to remember.

For extra safety, keep another backup away from home, such as a second external drive stored at a relative’s house or in a safe location. This helps protect against fire, flood, or theft.

Finally, check your backups every few months. Make sure files still open, cloud syncing is working, and your storage isn’t full. A backup is only useful if it is current and accessible.
