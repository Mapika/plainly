# Why You Should Read More Books

In an age of endless scrolling and 30-second videos, sitting down with a book can feel almost rebellious. But picking up a book might be one of the most rewarding habits you ever develop.

**Reading sharpens your mind.** Studies show that regular reading improves vocabulary, strengthens memory, and even slows cognitive decline. Unlike passive entertainment, books force your brain to actively engage—imagining characters, following arguments, and connecting ideas.

**Books expand your world.** A novel can drop you into 19th-century Russia or a distant galaxy. A memoir lets you live another person's life. Nonfiction hands you decades of expertise in a few hundred pages. Few experiences offer such a generous trade for your time.

**Reading builds empathy.** When you spend hours inside a character's thoughts, you learn to understand perspectives unlike your own. In a divided world, that's no small gift.

**It's also genuinely restful.** Just six minutes of reading has been shown to reduce stress more effectively than listening to music or taking a walk.

You don't need to commit to a book a week. Ten pages a day adds up to a dozen books a year. Start small—your future self will thank you.
