# How to Back Up Your Photos Safely

Losing precious photos to a broken phone or crashed hard drive is heartbreaking—but completely preventable. Follow these steps to keep your memories safe.

**1. Follow the 3-2-1 rule.** Keep at least three copies of your photos, on two different types of storage, with one copy stored offsite. This protects you from device failure, theft, and even house fires.

**2. Use cloud storage.** Services like Google Photos, iCloud, Dropbox, or Amazon Photos automatically back up images from your phone or computer. Enable auto-upload so new photos sync without you having to remember.

**3. Add a physical backup.** Copy your photo library to an external hard drive or SSD every few months. Store it somewhere safe, ideally in a different location than your computer.

**4. Organize as you go.** Sort photos into clearly labeled folders by year and event. This makes future backups easier and helps you find images quickly.

**5. Test your backups.** Occasionally open files from your backup to confirm they aren't corrupted and that you can actually recover them.

**6. Update regularly.** Set a calendar reminder to back up new photos monthly so nothing falls through the cracks.

A little effort now prevents major loss later.
