### **Composting for Beginners: Turn Scraps into Soil Gold**

Composting is nature’s way of recycling! It’s the simple process of turning your kitchen scraps and yard waste into a rich, nutrient-packed fertilizer for your plants.

**1. Choose Your Bin:** Start with a simple enclosed compost bin for your yard or a countertop tumbler. For small spaces, try a sealed bokashi bin or a worm farm (vermicomposting), which can even be done indoors.

**2. Know What to Add:** A healthy compost pile needs a balance of "greens" and "browns."
*   **Greens (Nitrogen):** Fruit/veggie scraps, coffee grounds, fresh grass clippings.
*   **Browns (Carbon):** Dried leaves, cardboard, straw, paper egg cartons, shredded newspaper.

**3. What to Avoid:** Never add meat, dairy, oils, or diseased plants, as they attract pests and create odors.

**4. Simple Steps:** Layer your greens and browns in your bin. Chop larger pieces to speed up decomposition. Keep the pile as moist as a wrung-out sponge and turn it with a pitchfork every week or two to add air.

In a few months, you’ll have dark, crumbly "black gold" to mix into your garden soil, helping your plants thrive while reducing landfill waste
