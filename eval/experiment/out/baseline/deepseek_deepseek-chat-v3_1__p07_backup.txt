### How to Back Up Your Photos Safely

Losing precious photos can be heartbreaking. To protect your memories, follow the "3-2-1 Backup Rule."

**1. The 3-2-1 Rule Explained**
This strategy means having **three** total copies of your data. Store them on **two** different types of media (e.g., your computer's hard drive and an external hard drive), with **one** copy kept off-site.

**2. Create Your Local Backups**
Start by transferring photos from your phone or camera to your computer. This is your first copy. Then, regularly back up that computer folder to an **external hard drive**. This is your second, separate copy, protecting you if your computer fails.

**3. Add an Off-Site Backup**
For crucial protection against theft, fire, or flood, use a **cloud storage service** like Google Photos, iCloud, or Dropbox. These services automatically sync your photos to secure remote servers, fulfilling the "off-site" requirement.

**Final Tip:** Make backing up a habit. Schedule a monthly reminder to update your external hard drive and check that your cloud service is running smoothly. With this simple system, your photos will be secure for years to come.
