# Why You Should Read More Books

We live in an age of infinite scroll. Our attention gets sliced into ever-thinner ribbons, and somewhere in the noise, the quiet pleasure of sitting with a book has started to feel almost old-fashioned. That's a shame, because few habits give back as much as reading does.

Books are the closest thing we have to time travel. Crack one open and you can stand in a medieval marketplace, argue philosophy with someone who died two thousand years ago, or live an entire life that isn't yours. That last part matters more than we admit. Studies keep finding that people who read fiction tend to be better at empathy. Spend enough hours inside other people's heads and you start to understand your own a little better too.

There's also the slow magic of focus. A book asks you to stay, to follow a single thread for hours instead of seconds. In a distracted world, that's a kind of training your mind quietly thanks you for.

You don't need to read a hundred books a year. Start with ten pages a night, one book that genuinely interests you. The habit builds itself. Your future self, calmer and a little wiser, will be grateful you began.
